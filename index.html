Flight Booking

How to Find the Cheapest Flights

The difference between paying £180 and £900 for the exact same seat almost always comes down to one thing: how and when you searched. Flight comparison platforms scan live fares from hundreds of airlines simultaneously, surfacing deals that would take hours to find manually — and in many cases, deals that never appear on airline websites at all.

The most powerful tool at your disposal is the flexible-date calendar. Shifting your departure by a single day can slash hundreds off a transatlantic fare. Combine that with fare alerts — which notify you the instant a route drops to your target price — and you'll consistently book at prices most travellers never see.

💡Pro tip: The cheapest fares typically appear 6–8 weeks before departure. Set an alert today, book when it triggers.

Airalo — eSIM Data

The Smartest Way to Stay Connected While Travelling

Airalo is the world's largest eSIM marketplace, and for good reason. With coverage across 200+ countries and territories, it gives travellers access to affordable local data plans before they even board their flight. You simply browse plans for your destination, purchase the one that fits your trip, scan a QR code, and you're online the moment your wheels touch the runway.

Plans are available for individual countries, regional bundles covering multiple destinations in one purchase, and global packages for frequent travellers who cross borders regularly. Data speeds are competitive — most plans run on 4G LTE or 5G networks — and the app makes managing multiple eSIM profiles genuinely simple.

💡Pro tip: Buy a regional bundle rather than a single-country plan if you're visiting multiple destinations. Airalo's Europe, Asia, and Middle East bundles typically work out 40–60% cheaper than buying separate plans for each country.

Every time you connect to hotel Wi-Fi, an airport lounge network, or a café hotspot, you are taking a risk. NordVPN wraps your entire internet connection in AES-256 encryption, the same standard used by governments and militaries worldwide, making your data completely unreadable to third parties.
